Cold Gad Propulsion Analysis and Design

The mission of the Cold Gas Propulsion Analysis and Design (CGPAD) project is to learn how to design and fabricate conical and parabolic nozzles for propulsion. The complexity lies in the design, as the size of the nozzle causes the boundary layer effects to become a dominant factor. The team will test a large number of nozzles. Each nozzle will have a certain pressure ratio and area ratio which will be plotted together for later nozzle designs. A 8th order polynomial fit will be assigned the plot; when other projects need a cold gas thruster nozzle, we can use this curve to get a good initial geometric estimation.


Our Propulsion System

Our propulsion system includes a regular, a solenoid, a tank, a nozzle, piping and interfaces all connected in series. The test stand includes a t-slot frame made entirely of aluminum. There is also an connected Arduino which will record the data from the load cell.


Our Team

Our team consists of RIT students from a multitude of majors and fields. Our multidisciplinary team serves to further our commitment to our academic mission. As a team we have held workshops for students on topics ranging from computer modeling with SOLIDWORKS to rocket theory and nozzle design.
